### Pre-Event To-Do List

- Set up an **eSIM**
- Download **Alipay** and link your credit/debit cards. We recommend having a minimum of $100 USD for emergencies + cost of bus to Shenzhen.
- Download **WeChat**
- Download this document (**File → Download → PDF**)
- Screenshot important information in this document
- **Google Maps will be disabled in China.** Download **Gaode Maps (Amap)** or **Baidu Maps** instead

#### About Paying

- **Alipay:** Link your debit/credit card — but some banks can't use it. Search online if your bank is compatible.
- **WeChat Pay:** Link your debit/credit card — check if your bank allows it.
- **Credit Card:** Major companies might accept international credit cards, but most won't.
- **Cash:** Get ~$100 USD equivalent in cash. Stores and restaurants must accept cash, but may not be able to give change.

### Flying to HKG

Staff will be waiting at the **Hong Kong International Airport (HKG)**.

Please **arrive before 4PM on July 1**, and depart **after 7PM on July 7** — or later, if you booked additional nights at the residence.

#### Airport Pickup

There will be staff at HKG on **July 1 from 10AM to 4PM**, standing right outside the departure hall. We'll have 3–4 group trips bringing folks from the airport to the accommodation (times announced in #soup-base and the WeChat group).

Heading to the venue by yourself is recommended and extremely straightforward. See our guide: **[Fallout] HKG to Accommodation**.

### Residence / Sleeping Arrangements

Rooms contain one queen bed. Participants will generally share one queen bed with one other participant. If you are uncomfortable sharing a bed, individual memory foam sleeping mats are available upon request.

---

### Internet / VPN

Many websites and apps (including Google, Gmail, Google Drive, Discord, Instagram, WhatsApp, etc.) **may not work in mainland China** without a VPN or a roaming SIM.

- We strongly recommend downloading and testing any VPN **before departure**.
- Please download this document, your arrival card, and all important documents before arriving.

## Part Three: Safety & Support

### Youth Protection Plan

Full-time Hack Club staff and volunteers are background checked and have safety training. All staff members are required to complete training related to this event and comply with our Child Safeguarding Policy. Training includes:

- Recognizing signs of abuse and neglect
- Appropriate and inappropriate behaviors
- Procedures for reporting suspected abuse or misconduct
- Strategies for maintaining a safe and inclusive environment

---

### Code of Conduct and Participant Behaviour

Hack Club Fallout follows our [Code of Conduct](https://hackclub.com/conduct). Kindness is a core Hack Club value — we expect participants to be kind, lead with empathy, and be proactive in helping others.

All participants are required to sign an agreement that they have read and will abide by the Code of Conduct.

Participants are explicitly prohibited from:
- Harassment, assault, battery, or sexual misconduct
- Possession or consumption of tobacco, e-cigarettes, alcohol, marijuana, or any other controlled substances, regardless of age or legality

Consequences for violations include:
- Removal from the event at the participant's own expense
- Cancellation of travel funding
- Ban from future Hack Club events or programs
- Notification of law enforcement if warranted
