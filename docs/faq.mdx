## Reviews & Approval

**How long do reviews take?**
We aim for under a week, but our reviewers are volunteers, so there's no guarantee. Just wait — we're getting through it. If you've been waiting **longer than 2 weeks**, let us know in [#fallout-help](https://hackclub.enterprise.slack.com/archives/C0ACJ290090) in case there's a bug.

**Can I get my review prioritized? (exams, components shipping, flight booked, etc.)**
No official expedited lane right now. As we approach the deadline, we expect to prioritize folks close to the 60-hour requirement so they can attend.

**Can someone pre-review my project before I submit?**
No official pre-review. You can ask in [#fallout](https://hackclub.enterprise.slack.com/archives/C037157AL30) for a sanity check from the community, or [#electronics](https://hackclub.enterprise.slack.com/archives/C056AMWSFKJ) for electronics-specific questions.

## Tracking Hours & What Counts

Read [How to Timelapse](/docs/project-resources/how-to-timelapse) first for the full DO / DON'T list.

**Does AI usage count toward hours?**
Quick lookups (syntax, part spec, a pinout question) are fine, but treat **30% as a hard ceiling** — your project needs to be yours, not AI's. AI-generated content (graphics, images, writing, code that you'd "heavily modify") is **not allowed** in the project itself.

**Does following a step-by-step tutorial count?**
No. You shouldn't be copying tutorials anyway — make meaningful changes to make it your own. Passive tutorial-watching doesn't count. Reference-checking a tutorial only counts when you're actively seeking specific info.

**Does time spent learning a new tool count? (KiCad, Python, Fusion…)**
Only when you're learning it **while applying it** to your project. Pre-learning a tool before touching your project doesn't count.

**Does writing firmware/software for my hardware project count?**
Yes. But you **cannot** submit the same time to any other program (Stasis, Forge, Macondo, Horizons, etc.) — that's double-dipping and is forbidden.

**Does time spent on my README / BOM / zine count?**
Yes, per the [DO track list](/docs/project-resources/how-to-timelapse#what-can-you-track). Normal AI/research rules still apply.

**Do 3D-printed parts / non-electronics projects count?**
"Hardware" just means physical — electronics aren't strictly required, but your project must orbit a clear technical goal.

- Mounts, enclosures, tools with a purpose — count.
- A plush with hardware components in it — counts (ask if unsure).
- 100 identical keychains with different logos — doesn't count, no purpose.
- Knitting a sweater — doesn't count, not engineering.

**What happens to idle / sleep time inside a recording?**
A human reviewer will remove it during review — it's not stripped automatically.

**I step out of frame to cut wires / grab a tool. Does that count?**
You don't need to show your face, but reviewers need to be able to tell **what** you're working on. Quick off-camera moments are fine — long unexplained gaps get deducted.

**Can I use AI to polish my English on the README / zine?**
No. It needs to be hand-written.

**Can I use AI-generated graphics for my zine?**
No. The zine must feel high-effort and designed. Fully AI-generated submissions can result in a permanent ban.

**Are Fallout's guided projects allowed to count as hours?**
Yes — guided projects are structured so everything you learn contributes directly to the final product. The project can't be a direct copy, though — make it your own.

**Can I record myself journaling / does that count?**
Yes.

**If I record today but journal tomorrow, which day does the time count for?**
The day the **journal entry is created**. Recording timestamp doesn't matter.

## Travel Stipends & Flights

See [Travel Stipends](/docs/travel/travel-stipends) for the full writeup.

**How much is the travel stipend?**

- **Koi (design hours)** → travel at **$5 / hour** (no build required)
- **Gold (after building IRL)** → travel at **$8.50 / hour** (better rate, requires a built project)
- **Need / merit-based flight stipend** → up to **$1,500 / person**, case-by-case

**Is the hourly stipend capped?**
No. The hourly stipend is separate from and additional to the $1,500 need/merit pool.

**When is the need-based flight stipend form open?**
Open now: [forms.hackclub.com/flyfallout](https://forms.hackclub.com/flyfallout). You do **not** need to have booked a flight yet — submitting flight _plans_ is fine. We consider both need and merit.

**Do I pay first and get reimbursed, or do you pay directly?**
Most people: pay first, reimbursed **after the event**. Special case-by-case exceptions for people who literally can't pay upfront — reach out.

**What does the stipend cover?**
Flights, trains, ferries directly to/from the event. Intra-China travel for event purposes is covered by us. Personal travel / sightseeing / cab rides for unplanned stuff — not covered.

**What if I book a flight and then don't qualify?**
That's a risk you take; unfortunately we can't reimburse. Note: you can still submit a project **after the event** and collect the hourly stipend after the fact.

## Streaks

**When does a "day" roll over?**
Midnight–midnight, in **your local timezone**.

**How much do I need to log to keep my streak?**
At least **1 hour** of recording attached to a journal entry created that day.

**How do streak freezes work?**

- Applied **automatically** when you miss a day — you can't opt out.
- You earn one at each milestone: **7, 14, 30, 60, 100 days**.
- You can hold a maximum of **5 freezes** at a time.

**What do I get from streak milestones & goals?**

- Milestones (7 / 14 / 30 / 60 / 100 days) → +1 freeze each.
- Optional **streak goals** you set yourself:
  - 3-day → 1 koi
  - 5-day → 2 koi
  - 7-day → 5 koi
  - 14-day → 10 koi
  - Goal completion also awards a freeze.

## Project Transfers

**How do I transfer a Blueprint project into Fallout?**
Fill out [hack.club/crashout](https://hack.club/crashout). It's manual, so no ETA. You can keep working in the meantime: create a project in Fallout under the **same name** and we'll backfill it.

**Do my Blueprint hours transfer 1:1?**
No. We manually review the Blueprint project and deflate hours to what we deem fair.

**Can I transfer an unfinished Blueprint project?**
Yes. Projects already built IRL in Blueprint cannot be transferred, but submitted **designs** (unbuilt) can move to Fallout so you can build them here.

**Can I transfer from Stasis?**
No — the Stasis team doesn't allow it.

**Can I submit the same project to Fallout AND another Hack Club program (Horizons, etc.)?**
No. Hours can only be submitted to one program. If you submit a project to Fallout, you can't submit it elsewhere. If you _don't_ submit it to Fallout, you're free to use it in another program.

## Timelapse Tools & Recording

See [How to Timelapse](/docs/project-resources/how-to-timelapse).

**Which tool should I use?**

1. **Lookout** (preferred)
2. **Lapse** if Lookout doesn't work
3. **YouTube / OBS** only as a last resort

**Camera mode vs screen recording?**

- **Screen recording** when you're working on your screen (CAD, coding).
- **Camera** when you're working in the real world (soldering, assembling).

**My YouTube livestream got taken down for copyrighted music — can I submit OBS logs?**
We need the video. Audio isn't required — you can re-upload silent.

**Can I switch tabs or windows mid-Lookout recording?**
Refresh the page or restart the app to switch what's being captured. The draft is saved up to the latest visible capture.

**Does Lookout save a draft if I exit mid-recording?**
Yes, up to the last visible capture.

**I timelapsed two projects in one recording — help?**
Leave a note explaining. This is rare — normally each timelapse should be one project.

**Is an unlisted YouTube upload OK?**
Yes.

**Should a YouTube upload be edited / sped-up, or raw?**
Raw. Do not pre-edit, speed up, or crop.

**My recording doesn't show a system clock — will it be rejected?**
Case-by-case. Docs require a visible clock; don't rely on leniency.

## BOM & Repo Requirements

See [Making a BOM](/docs/general-resources/making-a-bom) and [Submitting your Design](/docs/requirements/submitting-design).

**Do I include shipping in the BOM?**
Yes.

**How do I list 3D-printed parts / filament?**
Include them at their per-part material cost (pro-rated from a spool is fine).

**I already own a part — what do I put?**
Include it at its actual price for reproducibility and leave a note in the BOM. You can reimburse yourself if you have proof of purchase.

**What CAD files do you accept?**

- `.step` **plus** source file — `.f3d`, `.f3z`, FCStd, or a **public** Onshape link.
- **Blender does not count** as CAD.

**Do I need firmware to ship my design?**
Yes — if firmware is expected in the final build, it must be present for design ship. It can be minimal, but it must be a good-faith attempt that actually tries to work.

**My file is over 25 MB and GitHub refuses it.**
Use **Git LFS**. Files not in the repo aren't real. Only exception: a public, working Onshape link.

**EasyEDA: is `.eprj` + gerbers enough for the PCB source file requirement?**
Yes.

**How clean does my wiring diagram need to be?**
Use a schematic capture tool. Hand-drawn / "screenshot with lines" is not acceptable.

**Can I put LCSC links in my BOM even though LCSC shipping to my country is expensive?**
No. BOM links must point to vendors that actually work for **you** — you're the one building the project.
