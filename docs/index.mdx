## The Five Steps to _Fallout_

The premise is simple:

1. Ideate
2. Design digitally
3. Get funding
4. Build IRL
5. Share with the world (aka online)

And.. Go to Shenzhen\!

I’ll explain in a minute how getting money to build / going to Shenzhen works.

### Step 2: Track your progress (Journal \+ Timelapse)

Two months ago, Renran called at 1AM. The conversation went something like this:

"Okay. We want to help people build sickass hardware projects. But how do we make sure they’re real?"

<img src="/docs-assets/index/we-pray.webp" alt="Praying for real projects" style="width: 100%; max-width: 100%;" />

Unfortunately, we’ve been scammed before. Thousands of dollars given to fake projects. :\(

So… We settled on this: To get Koi (our currency), you’ll journal and timelapse your progress\!

#### TIMELAPSE

Screen record or film yourself through Lapse (Hack Club’s timelapse recording tool). It works in browser\!

If you’re feeling fancy, you can record or stream to YouTube with your own setup like OBS. Check out [How to Track Time](/docs/project-resources/how-to-timelapse) for more details\!

#### JOURNAL

Journal your day’s work by clicking on a trunk after you:

- Reached a milestone
- Finished for the day

Be detailed, write about what you did, what you learned, or how you failed.

<aside class="callout callout-info">

**AI doesn’t know you.** It doesn’t matter if you’re “bad at writing,” just don’t use it. We have a [Good Journaling](/docs/project-resources/good-journaling) page with some helpful tips\!

</aside>

<img src="/docs-assets/index/getting-koi.webp" alt="How to get koi" style="width: 100%; max-width: 100%;" />

You can exchange Koi Fish for hardware grants to build your project\!

### Step 3: Submit your design, get funding \- How to not get rejected

You might ask: "Why do you review my design?"

To this, I answer: "To help you make it work, you fool."

<img src="/docs-assets/index/doofus.webp" alt="Why we review" style="width: 100%; max-width: 100%;" />

At our busiest, we might take WEEKS to review projects. Save your time \-

<p class="text-xl! text-center text-red-600">
  <strong>95% of Rejections come from problems that take 5 Minutes to fix.</strong>
</p>

Check off the check list at [Submitting your Design](/docs/requirements/submitting-design) before submitting. You’ll need to make a good README, have all required files, and make a Zine poster.

<img src="/docs-assets/index/check-requirements.webp" alt="Check requirements" style="width: 100%; max-width: 100%;" />

After approval, we’re providing you 7 koi per hour ($5 USD) to buy parts\!\!

We’re a nonprofit with limited funds, so please be efficient with how you budget. See our [Project Funding](/docs/project-resources/project-funding) page to see how that works.

### Step 4: Buy, Build, Track — Where the Magic Happens

Okay. Now it’s time. Order your parts. It’s time to actually _build_ your project.

Be warned: your sleep schedule will look like this:

<img src="/docs-assets/index/no-sleep.webp" alt="No sleep" style="width: 100%; max-width: 100%;" />

We’re a nonprofit with limited funds, so please be efficient with how you spend. You’ll need to keep a BOM (Bill of Materials) at the end\! Check out [How to Buy Parts](/docs/general-resources/sourcing-parts)

Remember to track your progress and time spent building through timelapses and journals\!

Check out [How to Timelapse](/docs/project-resources/how-to-timelapse) & [Good Journaling](/docs/project-resources/good-journaling) for help\!
